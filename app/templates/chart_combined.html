      📋 What Each Pane Is Showing

      • Price + EMAs — Daily OHLC candles with five exponential moving averages (10/20/50/100/200) to read short-, medium-, and long-term trend. The background tint marks divergence-phase extremes (see below) so price action and relative strength can be read together without scanning down to a separate pane.
      • RS Ratio + Benchmark + Accumulation — Three indicators sharing one pane and one timeline. The RS Ratio family (right axis, green/blue/purple/amber) is stock price ÷ benchmark price — rising means the stock is gaining ground on the index; its 10 SMA / 21 EMA / 50 SMA confirm the trend isn't just noise. The Benchmark Index line (left axis, slate gray) is the raw S&P 500 / Nifty 500 level, shown on its own real scale so you can read overall market regime alongside relative strength. The Accumulation/Distribution line (bottom overlay, cyan) is a running total of volume on up-days minus volume on down-days (OBV-style) — its absolute level doesn't matter, only its slope: rising means net buying pressure, falling means net selling pressure. Reading them together: RS rising, the benchmark flat or falling, and Accumulation rising is the strongest combination — outperformance backed by real buying, not a market-wide rally lifting everything.
      • Background shading (both panes) — Blue tint = "True Alpha" divergence phase (RS rising while the benchmark falls). Red tint = "Flushing" phase (RS and the benchmark falling together). No tint = a mixed/neutral phase.
      • Green dot markers on price candles — RS Ratio has risen for 4 consecutive sessions — an early relative-strength uptrend tell, placed directly on price so it's never hidden in a collapsed pane.
