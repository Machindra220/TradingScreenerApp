      📋 What Each Pane Is Showing

      • RS Ratio Matrix — Stock price ÷ S&P 500 price. A rising line means the stock is gaining ground on the broad market regardless of whether the stock itself is up or down that day. The 10 SMA / 21 EMA / 50 SMA are smoothing speeds — short, medium, long — used to confirm the trend isn't just noise.
      • Benchmark Curve — The raw S&P 500 index level, plotted so you can read the overall market regime (risk-on / risk-off) alongside the stock's relative strength, without it being distorted by the stock's own price scale.
      • RS Divergence Phase — A four-state histogram comparing the 5-day slope of the RS ratio against the 5-day slope of the S&P. Blue bars = stock rising while the market falls (strongest signal); light blue = both rising but the stock faster; light red = stock lagging a rising market; dark red = both falling together.
      • Price + EMAs + Volume — Daily OHLC candles with five exponential moving averages (10/20/50/100/200) to read short-, medium-, and long-term trend, plus a volume histogram colored by up/down day to confirm whether moves are backed by participation.
